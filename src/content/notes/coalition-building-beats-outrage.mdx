---
title: "Coalition-building beats outrage"
summary: "The most useful civic tools are not just complaint funnels. They help people find shared interests and gather around practical proposals."
date: 2025-05-22
lang: "en"
---

Outrage is easy to generate. It travels fast, feels urgent, and fills comment sections. But outrage alone does not build anything. It signals a problem without creating the capacity to solve it.

The most useful civic tools operate on a different logic:

- They help people **discover shared interests** instead of amplifying disagreement.
- They make it easy to **gather around practical proposals** — even small, imperfect ones.
- They treat participation as **coordination**, not just expression.

A complaint funnel collects frustration. A coalition tool connects people who want the same outcome and gives them a way to move toward it together.

This is harder to build. It requires empathy for the user, restraint in feature design, and a willingness to ship something that feels modest but works reliably.

The result, though, is something that outlasts the news cycle. A tool that helps ten neighbors agree on a budget proposal is more durable than a platform that helps ten thousand people be angry about it.
